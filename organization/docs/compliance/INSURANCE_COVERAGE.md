## Overview

### Purpose

This document establishes Broxiva's insurance coverage requirements, ensuring adequate protection against operational, cyber, professional, and liability risks inherent to operating a global e-commerce marketplace platform.

### Objectives

- Define minimum insurance coverage requirements
- Establish coverage amount guidelines based on business scale
- Provide policy evaluation checklists
- Document insurance-related incident response procedures
- Ensure compliance with contractual and regulatory insurance requirements

### Insurance Program Principles

1. **Comprehensive Coverage** - Address all material risks to the business
2. **Adequate Limits** - Coverage amounts aligned with potential exposure
3. **Quality Carriers** - Partner with financially stable, reputable insurers
4. **Regular Review** - Annual assessment of coverage adequacy
5. **Claims Readiness** - Clear procedures for incident reporting and claims

## Required Insurance Types

### 1. Cyber Liability Insurance

**Purpose:** Protection against losses from cyber incidents, data breaches, and technology failures.

#### Coverage Components

| Component | Description | Priority |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **First-Party Coverage** | | |
| Data Breach Response | Costs of breach notification, credit monitoring, forensics | Required |
| Business Interruption | Lost income during cyber incidents | Required |
| Data Recovery | Costs to restore/recreate lost data | Required |
| Cyber Extortion | Ransomware payments and response costs | Required |
| Reputation Management | PR and crisis management expenses | Recommended |
| **Third-Party Coverage** | | |
| Privacy Liability | Claims from affected data subjects | Required |
| Network Security Liability | Claims from security failures | Required |
| Media Liability | Claims from online content | Recommended |
| Regulatory Defense | Costs of regulatory investigations | Required |
| PCI DSS Fines | Payment card industry penalties | Required |

#### Key Policy Features to Require

- [ ] Retroactive coverage date (ideally inception of business)
- [ ] Coverage for cloud-hosted data
- [ ] Coverage for vendor/third-party breaches
- [ ] No exclusion for unencrypted data
- [ ] Coverage for social engineering fraud
- [ ] Coverage for cryptocurrency-related incidents
- [ ] Worldwide coverage territory
- [ ] 24/7 breach response hotline
- [ ] Panel of pre-approved vendors (forensics, legal, PR)

#### Exclusions to Negotiate/Avoid

- War/terrorism exclusions (review carefully)
- Infrastructure failure exclusions
- Unpatched software exclusions
- Prior knowledge exclusions
- Contractual liability exclusions

### 3. Directors and Officers (D&O) Insurance

**Purpose:** Protection for directors, officers, and the company against claims alleging wrongful acts in their capacity as leaders.

#### Coverage Components (ABC Structure)

| Side | Coverage | Description | Priority |
|------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Side A | Individual Directors/Officers | When company cannot indemnify | Required |
| Side B | Corporate Reimbursement | Reimburses company for indemnification | Required |
| Side C | Entity Coverage | Direct coverage for entity (securities claims) | Required |

#### Key Policy Features to Require

- [ ] Broad definition of "Insured Persons"
- [ ] Coverage for employment practices claims
- [ ] Coverage for fiduciary liability
- [ ] Coverage for regulatory investigations
- [ ] Advancement of defense costs
- [ ] No exclusion for private company securities
- [ ] Coverage for pre-IPO activities (if applicable)
- [ ] Outside directorship coverage

#### D&O for Startups/Growth Companies

- [ ] Coverage for investor claims
- [ ] Coverage for M&A-related claims
- [ ] Coverage for employee claims (misrepresentation in hiring)
- [ ] Coverage for customer claims (if not covered elsewhere)
- [ ] Runoff coverage provisions

## Incident Response Insurance Procedures

### Cyber Incident Response

#### Step 1: Initial Response (0-4 Hours)

```markdown
1. CONTAIN the incident (technical team)
2. DO NOT admit liability or make statements
3. NOTIFY the following:
   - [ ] Incident Response Team Lead
   - [ ] Legal Counsel
   - [ ] CFO/Insurance Contact
   - [ ] CEO (if major incident)

4. DOCUMENT everything:
   - Time incident discovered
   - How incident was discovered
   - Initial scope assessment
   - Actions taken
```

#### Step 2: Insurance Notification (4-24 Hours)

```markdown
1. GATHER information for carrier:
   - [ ] Date/time of incident discovery
   - [ ] Type of incident (breach, ransomware, etc.)
   - [ ] Initial scope estimate
   - [ ] Systems affected
   - [ ] Data types potentially affected
   - [ ] Actions taken to contain

2. CONTACT insurance:
   - Carrier: [CARRIER NAME]
   - Claims Hotline: [PHONE NUMBER]
   - Email: [EMAIL ADDRESS]
   - Policy Number: [POLICY NUMBER]

3. ENGAGE panel vendors (with carrier approval):
   - [ ] Forensics firm
   - [ ] Breach counsel
   - [ ] Crisis communications (if needed)
   - [ ] Notification vendor (if needed)
```

#### Step 3: Claims Management (Ongoing)

```markdown
1. MAINTAIN communication with carrier:
   - Provide regular status updates
   - Obtain approval for significant expenses
   - Document all costs for reimbursement

2. TRACK expenses:
   - [ ] Forensics costs
   - [ ] Legal costs
   - [ ] Notification costs
   - [ ] Credit monitoring costs
   - [ ] Business interruption losses
   - [ ] Regulatory defense costs
   - [ ] PR/crisis management costs

3. PRESERVE evidence:
   - [ ] Logs and forensic images
   - [ ] Communication records
   - [ ] Decision documentation
   - [ ] Expense receipts
```

### Professional Liability/E&O Incident Response

#### Step 1: Claim/Potential Claim Identification

```markdown
Potential claim indicators:
- Customer complaint threatening legal action
- Demand letter received
- Lawsuit filed
- Regulatory inquiry
- Significant platform failure affecting customers
```

#### Step 2: Immediate Actions

```markdown
1. DO NOT:
   - Admit fault or liability
   - Make promises or commitments
   - Discuss with external parties
   - Destroy or alter any documents

2. DO:
   - [ ] Notify Legal immediately
   - [ ] Preserve all relevant documents
   - [ ] Document the situation
   - [ ] Limit internal discussions
```

#### Step 3: Insurance Notification

```markdown
1. NOTIFY carrier within policy timeframe (typically 30 days)

2. PROVIDE:
   - [ ] Description of claim/potential claim
   - [ ] Claimant information
   - [ ] Relevant contracts/agreements
   - [ ] Timeline of events
   - [ ] Potential exposure estimate
   - [ ] Related documents

3. COORDINATE with carrier on:
   - [ ] Selection of defense counsel
   - [ ] Response strategy
   - [ ] Settlement authority
```

### Appendix A: Insurance Terminology Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Aggregate Limit** | Maximum amount insurer will pay during policy period |
| **Claims-Made** | Coverage triggered when claim is made (vs. when incident occurred) |
| **Deductible/Retention** | Amount insured pays before coverage applies |
| **Duty to Defend** | Insurer's obligation to provide defense |
| **Endorsement** | Modification to policy terms |
| **Exclusion** | Specific situations not covered |
| **Hammer Clause** | Settlement consent provision |
| **Occurrence** | Coverage triggered when incident occurs |
| **Prior Acts** | Coverage for incidents before policy inception |
| **Retroactive Date** | Earliest date for which coverage applies |
| **Subrogation** | Insurer's right to pursue third parties |
| **Tail Coverage** | Extended reporting period after policy ends |
